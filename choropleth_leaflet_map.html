How much CO2 does an average citizen produce?

Greenhouse gasses (GHGs) play a huge role in climate change. When present in the atmosphere, these gases trap radiation in the form of heat, causing a warming process called the greenhouse effect. Of the many types of GHGs, carbon dioxide (CO2) is the type that humans have the most control over thus the greatest ability to curtail.

Often when climate change and GHGs are discussed it is presented in terms that are too large for most people to comprehend. Using data obtained from the United Nations and the Leaflet JavaScript library, this map seeks to provide viewers an easier-to-understand and personal view of the amount of CO2 the average citizen in each country emits per year.

For reference, in 2013 the global average was approximately 5 metric tons per person per year.

Of the 231 countries and territories identified by the UN, 198 reported data on CO2 emissions and each of those countries has been ranked in terms of their average CO2 emissions per person (1-198).
